# Product-Sales-and-Pricing-Analysis
Product sales and pricing analysis focused on understanding customer purchasing behavior, identifying profitable price ranges, evaluating sales trends, and assessing the impact of pricing adjustments using Excel to support better business and financial decisions.

## Overview
This project focuses on analyzing product sales data to uncover pricing trends, sales performance, and profitability insights that support financial and business decision-making.

The analysis was conducted using Excel and focused on evaluating sales behavior across different pricing ranges, daily sales performance, and the impact of price adjustments on customer purchasing patterns.

---

## Analysis Objectives

The analysis focused on:
- Overall sales value for each day  
- Average sales value per day  
- Rounded sales values to the nearest 5 rupees and comparison with actual sales values  
- Sales performance across different price buckets:
  - 300–500 rupees  
  - 500–700 rupees  
  - 700–900 rupees  
  - 900–1100 rupees  
  - 1100–1300 rupees  
- Number of products sold within each price range  
- Comparison between product sales value and average selling price  

---

## Key Insights

### Highest Selling Product
- Product PIZB0002 emerged as the highest-selling product overall.

### Highest Sales Day
- The highest sales day recorded was **22nd of June 2022**, generating a total sales value of **20,243 rupees**.

### Preferred Price Range
- The **300–500 rupees** category recorded the highest purchase volume, with a total of **235 products sold**.
- This indicates that customers showed stronger preference for products within this pricing range.

### Most Profitable Price Range
- The **700–900 rupees** category generated the highest revenue, contributing a total sales value of **186,171 rupees**.
- Although fewer products were sold within this range compared to lower price categories, it delivered stronger profitability.

### Price Adjustment Analysis
- A comparison was conducted between actual sales values and prices rounded to the nearest 5 rupees.
- The analysis indicated that customer demand would likely remain favorable even after slight price increases.
